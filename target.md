# Clarmi Design Studio — Target Industries

## Why Not Restaurants

Restaurants use all-in-one POS platforms (Toast, Resy, Square) that bundle ordering, reservations, and a basic website. Even if the site is ugly, they won't pay for a standalone site because "it works." Low conversion, not worth pursuing.

## Top Tier

### 1. Home Services (plumbers, HVAC, roofers, electricians, landscapers)

- No all-in-one platform exists. Jobber/Housecall Pro handle scheduling but don't bundle real websites — just garbage landing pages
- Already paying $500-2K/mo for SEO leads on Angi/Thumbtack. A $1K site + $25/mo is a rounding error
- Every job is $500-15K+ so one lead from the site pays for years of Clarmi
- Massive volume — every city has hundreds of them with terrible or no sites
- Adventure mode goldmine — tons have zero web presence

### 2. Med Spas / Aesthetics Clinics

- No all-in-one platform. Separate booking (Vagaro, Boulevard) + separate site
- Spend heavily on marketing ($3-10K/mo on ads). $1K for a site is nothing
- Visual industry — scroll-video sites actually matter to them
- Average customer lifetime value is $2-5K so they understand paying for presence

### 3. Solo/Small Law Firms (injury, family, immigration, criminal defense)

- Legal marketing agencies charge $3-10K+ for a site. Clarmi is 10x cheaper
- No bundled platform — Clio does case management, not websites
- One case can be worth $5-50K. They'll pay for credibility
- Extremely search-dependent ("lawyer near me")

## Strong Tier

### 4. Auto Repair / Body Shops / Detailing

- Universally terrible sites. No platform lock-in
- Trust-dependent (people Google before handing over their car)
- Average ticket $500-3K. They can afford it

### 5. Dental / Small Medical Practices

- Currently overpaying template vendors like PatientPop ($300-500/mo for cookie-cutter sites)
- Clarmi is cheaper AND better looking. Easy pitch: "you're paying $500/mo for a template"

### 6. Veterinary Clinics

- Emotional purchase, outdated sites, no bundled platform
- Similar to dental — overpaying for mediocre vendor sites

## Recommended Priority

### High Priority (active targeting)

1. **Med spas / aesthetics clinics** — higher-end clients who care about aesthetics, appreciate scroll-video quality
2. **Solo/small law firms** — high willingness to pay, credibility-driven
3. **Veterinary clinics** — emotional niche, outdated competition
4. **Auto repair / body shops / detailing** — easy wins, universally bad sites

### Lower Priority (opportunistic)

5. **Home services** — largest market, lowest sophistication, easiest close, no competing platform
6. **Dental** — price displacement play against overpriced vendors
